Elephant

African elephants are found in 37 countries in sub-Sahara Africa, with an estimated 70% in Southern Africa, 20% in Eastern Africa, 6% in Central Africa and 3% in West Africa.

  • Scientific Name : Loxodonta
  • Average Length: 8.2 to 13 feet (2.5 to 4 meters)
  • Average Lifespan : 70 years
  • Habitat: Tropical

Elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth. Two species of elephant, the Asian elephant and African elephant, remain extant. Elephants are herbivores and prefer habitats that give them access to food and water. Both species consume as many as 300 pounds of food per day.
